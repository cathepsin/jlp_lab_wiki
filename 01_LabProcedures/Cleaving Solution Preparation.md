# Cleaving Solution Preparation
## Summary
Method for preparing solution required for cleaving a peptide from [[Rink Amide]] resin

## Materials
- Glass dram vial
	- Plastic microcentrifuge tube for micro scale
- Syringes
- Pipettes
## Reagents
- [[Phenol]]
- Milli-Q (mQ) Water
- [[Triisopropylsilane]]
- [[1,2-Ethanedithiol]]
- [[Thioanisole]]
- [[Trifluoroacetic Acid]]
\* *[[#Reagent Amounts|See notes]] for reagent amounts* 

## Procedure
1. Add weighed [[Phenol|phenol]] to a dram vial 
2. Add Milli-Q water, [[Triisopropylsilane]], [[1,2-Ethanedithiol]], and [[Thioanisole]] to the dram vial
3. Carefully ([[#Trifluoroacetic Acid Safety|*see notes*]]) add [[Trifluoroacetic Acid||trifluoroacetic acid]] to the dram vial and vortex until everything is incorporated

### [[Trifluoroacetic Acid]] Safety

[[Trifluoroacetic Acid]] (or TFA) is an extremely dangerous strong organic acid. Do not handle this chemical alone and ensure you are wearing appropriate protection. This should include elbow-length neoprene gloves and a face shield on top of splash-proof goggles and other standard PPE.
